### Team Work and What I Learned
**Game Off 2024**
Situation
- I worked in a team of 4 for the "Game Off 2024" game jam

Task
- Our goal was to be able to build a complete game from scratch, and deliver it before a strict deadline of 30 days. 

Action
- During the first day, I stepped in to organize the group. I am close friends with each of the members, so I delegated tasks to each member, based on their strengths. One member was tasked with designing the looks, appearance, and the interface, another member was the background music and sound effects, whilst the last member and I focuses on developing the core programming and logic

Result
- We were able to successfully deliver the game on time, and we placed 115 out of 509 entries. 
- From this, I learned how to assign tasks based on each of my teammates strengths, which is a skill I want to bring to this summer placement

### Teamwork, Something Didn't Go to Plan

### Delivering Good Customer Service in Busy Environment
**Topbun**
Situation
- I work as a chef at Top Bun, which is a fast paced burger and fries kitchen.

Task
- During a rush, a customer called in for a customized off-menu burger. They wanted to add in the cheese sauce we used for our nachos, as well as adding an extra patty to their burger.

Action
- Because that day was quite busy, I immediately called my manager to figure out what was possible, concerning like the combination of those toppings as well as if there would be an extra charge. I then went back to the customer and explained what were were able to do, the extra charge, and we came to a mutual understanding.

### Putting Myself in Customer's Shoes
**Royal Park**
Situation
- I worked as a waiter/food server at a Chinese restaurant called Royal Park, where I managed food collection and customer seating.

Task
- During a shift, the kitchen was backed up with orders, and I noticed a seated customer that had been waiting for their food for quite some time. They were getting visibly frustrated, hands tapping on the table, and legs shaking.

Action
- So I put myself in their shoes, and knew that they were most likely tired and hungry, the worst combo. So instead of ignoring them, I went over to apologize for the wait, and personally went into the kitchen to find out what was holding up their order. I came back to them, and explained the situation, giving them a realistic time frame on when it would be ready. I then offered them a complimentary drink while they waited.

Result
- The customer immediately calmed down, and seemed more relaxed and understanding for the wait. I learned that showing empathy and really putting myself in their shoes, can help turn a negative experience for them, into a more positive experience one.

### Projects and Personal Achievements
**Arch**

During out brainstorm, we had a huge list of features we wanted to implement, and I was determined to make them all fit within the limited storage space.

Result
- In the end, I was able to successfully store everything from ou initial brainstowm, using up over 95% of the total memory. I am very proud of this project, as it shows my ability to take a difficult technical restrait, research research for solutions, and push the chip to its limit.
