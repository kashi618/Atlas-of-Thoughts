## Local Area Networks
Private communications networks
- Used to interconnect computing resources within an organisation
- All devices attach to a shared transmission medium

### Types of LANs
**PC LANs** (aka the Access Layer)
- Used to connect low-cost devices such as PC's and small servers
- Cheap to implement and maintain, but consequently their performance and speed is low

**Backend LANs** (aka the Core Layer)
- Used to connect high-end devices such as mainframes, supercomputers, mass storage devices
- Mainly used for bulk transfer of data
- Costly to implement and maintain, but consequently their performance and speed is high

**Backbone LANs** (aka the Distribution Layer)
- Used to provide connectivity between PC LANs and Backend LANs

## LAN Characteristics
Data transmitted in blocks known as frames
- Each frame contains data, plus control information (similar to HDLC)
Stations must gain access to the shared transmission medium.
- Access must be provided on a fair and equitable basis. i.e, there must be a control mechanism
