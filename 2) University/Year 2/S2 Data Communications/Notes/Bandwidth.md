## Bandwidth
Bandwidth is the range of frequencies within a signal. It can also be known as the capacity of a transmission system. It is found by subtracting the lowest from the highest frequency.
- As we increase the bandwidth (frequency), we increase its accuracy
![[Bandwidth.png|300]]

## Absolute Bandwidth
Describes the entire frequency range a perfect signal would occupy.
This is the total width of a signal's frequency spectrum. It is found by subtracting the lowest form the highest frequency. It is the theoretical/mathematical concept, as it describes what a perfect signal would copy without error. For digital signals, the bandwidth would be infinite due to its square shape.
- If a signal's frequency range is between `f` and `5f`, its absolute bandwidth is `4f`

## Effective Bandwidth
Effective bandwidth is a more practical approach to bandwidth. Because we can't have "infinite" frequencies, we instead use a finite range containing enough frequencies for it to be usable.
