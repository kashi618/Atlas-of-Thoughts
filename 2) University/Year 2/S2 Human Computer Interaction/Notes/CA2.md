## My Project
**Summary of User Research**
During CA1, my group and I conducted research on the irish website "Revenue.ie".

We focused on understanding people's experiences, when filing their annual tax returns online.

We gathered lots of research about revenue.ie, such as personal experiences from each member in my group, and user reviews on various websites such as Trustpilot, Google Reviews, Reddit, and Glassdoor.ie.

From this research, we discovered that a large majority of the users are either confused by, annoyed, or disappointed with the layout and navigation of the website.
This is seen especially with those with English literacy skills.

**Problem Identification**
Due to the large amount of operations within revenue.ie, I have decided to focus purely on the general layout, and more specifically the PAYE services. This is because the majority of the people who use revenue.ie, often use it for managing their taxes, paychecks, and employment details. This is also the area I am most familiar with, allowing me to find first-hand issues that I myself have encountered.

First of all, revenue.ie splits its services into 6 distinct sections, each colour coded with icons. The PAYE section of revenue.ie stays at the very top of the website, which believe was done purposefully, as it is the most commonly accessed service.

Upon first interaction it seems organised. However, as soon as you try to access the PAYE section, you will quickly learn that it is not the case.  

The first issue comes from accessing each option within the PAYE section. Each option is separated by blue text links, yet they are placed very close to each other, almost with no spacing between them. This makes it very difficult to differentiate and find where each option starts and ends. 

These links are also very verbose and unstructured. For example, the first two links are about managing your taxes, then it goes into job and pension details, then into pay and taxes again, and back to job/empoyment details.
