## Forming a contract
**Ad Idem**
"Of one mind"
- Both parties must be agreeing to the same thing at the same time

**Invitation to Treat**
Not an offer, instead it is an invitation for you to make an offer. E.G. price tag in shop window

**Counter Offer**
A response to an offer, that changes the terms. Legally this invalidates the original offer, and can't go back to it unless the offeror agres

**Postal Rule**
A legal quirk where an acceptance to an offer through mail is binding, the moment the letter is dropped INTO the post box NOT what it's received. This means that if you accept, but the letter was lost through mail, the offer is still valid and binding

**Promissory Estoppel**
A shield that allows a promise to be enforced, even if it lacks the elements of a contract. It prevents a promisor from going back on their promise.

## Contract Terms
**Condition**
The fundamental term. If this is broken, then then contract is dead and you can sue for damages

**Warranty**
A minor term. If broken, you can still sue for damages. However, you still need to finish the contract

**Innomiate Term**
An intermediate term, where remedy depends on how much "mess" the contract breach caused

**Expressed and Implied**
Expressed terms are what is said or wrote.
Implied terms are "read into" the contract, either by law or custom

**Contra Proferentem**
A rue rule that if the term is value, the court interprets it against the person who wrote the contract

## Other Factors
**Caveat Emptor**
"Buyer beware". A seller doesn't have to point out every flaw in a product, unless you ask

**Operative Mistake**
A fundamental error in a contract, that goes to the very heart of the agreement, making it void or invalid

**Misrepresentation**
A false statement, that tricks someone into a contract. Can be innocent, negligent, or fraudulent

**Ex Turpi Non Oritur**
You cannot sue someone for breaking a contract to do something illegal. E.G. a hitman failing to do his job
